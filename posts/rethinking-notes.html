Rethinking how I take notes

Sep 2025

For most of my career I took notes the way I was taught to in school: comprehensive, organised, filed away. I rarely looked at them again. The act of writing felt productive. The notes themselves were not.

I've spent the last year experimenting with a different approach, and I think I finally understand what went wrong.

Notes are not storage

I was using notes as an external memory â€” a place to put things so I didn't have to hold them in my head. The problem is that a note you never re-read provides exactly the same value as a note you never took. Storage without retrieval is just filing.

Write less, revisit more

The shift that actually helped was taking fewer notes and reading them more often. One short note I return to weekly is worth more than ten thorough ones I never see again. The bottleneck is not capture, it is integration.

What I do now

A single file per week. Whatever felt worth keeping. Sunday evening I read it, pull out anything still relevant, and archive the rest. The system has no categories, no tags, no hierarchy. It has one question: does this still matter?
